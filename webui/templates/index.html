    How Gatecrash Works

    Gatecrash sits between your devices and your router, transparently routing selected devices through a WireGuard VPN — without any configuration changes on those devices.

    It uses ARP spoofing to intercept traffic: it tells your target devices that it is the router, and tells the router it is the target devices. Traffic flows through Gatecrash and is forwarded via the VPN tunnel.

    Only devices you explicitly enable are affected. Everything else uses your normal internet connection.

    Getting Started

    1. Upload your WireGuard .conf file in Config → VPN Config
    2. Scan the network and add the devices you want to route through the VPN
    3. Enable the devices you want active using the toggles
    4. Press the power button to start — enabled devices will route through the VPN

    Auto-Stop

    Auto-stop monitors traffic for each enabled device. If a device drops below the traffic threshold for longer than the idle timeout, it is automatically disabled — useful for devices left on overnight after streaming.

    Configure global settings in Config → Auto-Stop. You can exempt individual devices from auto-stop in the device info popup (tap the i button on any saved device).

    Auto-stopped devices show as disabled in the device list. Just re-enable them to start routing again.

    Audit Log

    All service actions are logged to /var/log/gatecrash.log and visible on the Diagnostics tab. This includes service start/stop, login attempts, device changes, config updates, reboots, and auto-stop events.

    The log auto-refreshes every 10 seconds and rotates at 2 MB.

    Troubleshooting

    Devices not routing through VPN? Check the Diagnostics tab — confirm arpspoof processes are running and the vpntarget route is present.

    ARP spoofing not working? On a client device, run arp -a (Windows) or arp -n (Mac/Linux) and check that the router's IP shows Gatecrash's MAC address.

    WireGuard won't start? Check your .conf file has a valid PrivateKey and Endpoint.

    Something restarting unexpectedly? Check the audit log on the Diagnostics tab — it shows exactly what triggered each start/stop, including systemd restarts and IP watchdog events.

    "Not Secure" Browser Warning

    Your browser may show a "Not secure" or "Your connection is not private" warning when connecting to Gatecrash. This is normal and expected.

    Gatecrash uses a self-signed TLS certificate to encrypt all traffic between your browser and the device. The connection is fully encrypted — passwords, settings, and VPN keys cannot be intercepted on the network. The warning appears only because the certificate wasn't issued by a public certificate authority, which isn't possible for a local network device.

    To proceed past the warning:

    • Chrome / Edge: Click Advanced, then Proceed to <address>
    • Firefox: Click Advanced, then Accept the Risk and Continue
    • Safari: Click Show Details, then visit this website

    You only need to do this once per browser. The warning will not reappear after you accept.
